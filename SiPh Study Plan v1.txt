Silicon Photonics Learning Agenda – Comprehensive version

Part I: Foundations
1.1. Module 1: Why Silicon Photonics? Motivation and overview
	What is silicon phonics? Key components of Photonics Integrated Circuits (PICs).
	Comparison with electronics: speed, power, bandwidth, CMOS compatibility
	Key Applications: AI interconnects, Telecommunications, Light Detection and Ranging (LiDAR), Sensing, 
	Current market players and industry trends

1.2. Module 2: Basic Optics & Wave Theory
	Electromagnetic waves, refractive index, dispersion
	Reflection, refraction, total internal reflection (TIR)
	Polarization (TE/TM) – important for devices
	O‑band vs. C‑band: wavelength windows, attenuation (signal loss), chromatic dispersion

1.3. Module 3: Waveguide Platforms & Materials – The Foundation of PICs
1.3.1. Waveguide properties
	What is a waveguide? Categories of waveguides (slab, strip, rib, ridge, slot and diffused waveguide)
	Optical waveguide modes: single‑mode vs. multi‑mode
	Confinement factor and effective index
	Waveguide loss mechanism: scattering, absorption, bending loss
	Waveguide primary loss metrics: Insertion loss (IL), propagation loss (dB/cm or dB/mm), coupling loss, return loss 
	Waveguide loss measurement methods: cutback method, direct Insertion Loss method, Fabry-Perot (FP) method, all-pass ring resonator method

1.3.2. Waveguide materials
	Silicon‑on‑insulator (SOI): high index, low loss, CMOS compatible
	Silicon nitride (SiNx): low loss, wide transparency, high optical power handling
	Lithium niobate on insulator (LNOI): ferroelectric, high damage threshold, electro‑optic modulators
	Other materials: Heterogeneous integration (III‑V bonding, micro‑transfer printing µTP)
	Material selection trade‑offs: performance vs. manufacturability

 
Part II: Passive Components
2.1. Module 4: Basic Passive Devices – Couplers, Interferometer & Filters
	Directional couplers: coupled mode equations
	Mach‑Zehnder interferometer (MZI): principle, splitting ratio, phase shift, combining (cover more in Module 7)
	Y‑branches splitter, multimode interference couplers (MMIs)
	Ring resonators: Microring, Racetrack resonator, FSR, Q‑factor, critical coupling, filter/switch application (cover more in Module 7)
	Comparison: MZI vs. ring for filtering, switching, modulation

2.2. Module 5: Polarization Controller
	Why polarization control matters in PICs?
	Polarization splitter‑rotator (PSR)
	Polarization‑diverse designs (e.g., Dual-Polarization Waveguide Receiver)
	Manufacturing sensitivity: extinction ratio vs. fabrication tolerances

2.3. Module 6: Advanced WDM Components (Optional)
	Arrayed waveguide grating (AWG): design, crosstalk, channel spacing
	Waveguide echelle grating (WEG), Waveguide Interleaver
	Ring‑based WDM
	MUX/DEMUX using cascaded MZIs or cascaded rings
 
Part III: Active Components & System Building Blocks
3.1. Module 7: Modulators – Physics & Variants
	Plasma dispersion effect in Si (carrier depletion vs. injection)
	MZI modulator (with thermal switch/phase shifter): push‑pull configuration
	Ring modulator: trade‑offs
	Modulator metrics: extinction ratio, VπL, bandwidth (GHz), optical loss, speed
	Alternative materials: LiNbO3 (LNOI) modulators, Si‑organic hybrid (SOH)

3.2. Module 8: Optical Switches & Routing
	Thermo‑optic switches (Δn/ΔT)
	Electro‑optic switches (carrier-depletion effect/nonlinear effect)
	MEMS switches, optical switch matrix
	Switch metrics: speed, efficiency, insertion loss

3.3. Module 9: Photodetectors & Lasers (Optional)
	Ge photodetector: Why Ge-on-Si? (absorption at O/C band, responsivity, speed), Ge/Si Avalanche Photodiodes (APDs)
	VCSELs (off‑chip for short reach) vs. edge‑emitters <Fabry-Perot (FP) lasers, Distributed Feedback (DFB) lasers, and Electro-absorption Modulated Lasers (EMLs)>
	Semiconductor optical amplifiers (SOAs): on‑chip gain, signal loss compensation

 
Part IV: System Integration & Fiber Interface
4.1. Module 10: Fiber‑to‑Chip Coupling
	Grating couplers (vertical): polarization‑dependent, narrowband
	Edge couplers & spot‑size converters (SSC): polarization-insensitive, low loss, wideband

4.2. Module 11: Electronic–Photonic Co‑Design
	Electronic Photonic Integrated Circuits (EPICs): CMOS-photonic integration
	Design for manufacturing: electrical‑optical co‑simulation

Part V: Advanced Topics & Applications
5.1. Module 12: Nonlinear & Thermal Optics (Optional)
5.1.1. Thermal Optics
	Thermo‑optic effect: heaters, tuners, athermal designs
	Thermal management: crosstalk, heat sinking
5.1.2. Nonlinear Optics
	Nonlinear effects in Si/SiNx: Kerr, two‑photon absorption, four‑wave mixing
	Nonlinear effects in LNOI: Second-Harmonic Generation (SHG), Sum/Difference Frequency Generation (SFG/DFG), Optical Parametric Oscillation/Amplification (OPO/OPA)
	Quasi-Phase Matching (QPM): periodically poled lithium niobate (PPLN), poling mechanism
